# COLREGS Training Categories

10 categories for comprehensive STCW Table A-II/1 coverage:

## 1. lights
Night identification of vessel types by light configurations (Rules 20-31).
Example: "You see two all-round red lights in a vertical line. What vessel?"

## 2. day_shapes
Day identification by shapes displayed (Rules 20-31).
Example: "A vessel displays ball-diamond-ball. What does this indicate?"

## 3. encounters
Rules 13-15 encounter classification and correct action.
Example: "Target vessel bearing 010 relative, similar speed, reciprocal course. What situation?"

## 4. sound_signals_maneuvering
Rule 34 signals when vessels are in sight of one another.
- 1 short: altering to starboard
- 2 short: altering to port
- 3 short: operating astern propulsion
- 5+ short: danger signal
- 2 prolonged + 1 short: overtaking on your starboard
- 2 prolonged + 2 short: overtaking on your port

## 5. sound_signals_fog
Rule 35 fog signals.
- 1 prolonged / 2min: power-driven making way
- 2 prolonged / 2min: power-driven stopped
- 1 prolonged + 2 short / 2min: NUC, RAM, CBD, sailing, fishing, towing
- 1 prolonged + 3 short: vessel being towed
- Rapid bell 5sec / 1min: at anchor (<100m)
- Bell forward + gong aft: at anchor (100m+)

## 6. restricted_visibility
Rule 19 — replaces Rules 11-18 when vessels cannot see each other.
No give-way/stand-on. Radar-based action decisions.

## 7. tss
Rule 10 — Traffic Separation Schemes.
Joining, leaving, crossing at right angles. Small vessel/fishing obligations.

## 8. narrow_channels
Rule 9 — Keep to starboard. Overtaking sound signal exchange.
Obligations of vessels <20m, sailing, fishing.

## 9. general_rules
Rules 2-8 — Lookout (R5), safe speed (R6), risk of collision (R7), action to avoid collision (R8).

## 10. responsibilities
Rule 18 hierarchy: NUC > RAM > CBD > fishing > sailing > power-driven.
"Who gives way to whom?"
